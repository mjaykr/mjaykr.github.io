About Me

I am a dedicated Postdoctoral Research Associate with a strong background in Materials Science and Engineering. My current research at the University of Sheffield focuses on the mechanical properties of battery materials, where I fabricate and characterize complex metal oxides for next-generation battery technologies. I have extensive experience with in-situ nano-indentation, EBSD, and micro-computed tomography. Previously, at the University of Manchester, I worked on modeling the formability of light alloys, extending crystal plasticity models to better understand material behavior during warm forming processes. My Ph.D. from IIT Kanpur involved an in-depth study of the deformation behavior of tungsten heavy alloys using both experimental techniques and crystal plasticity simulations.
